COVert

A watch-based application that helps to educate, protect, and empower users in addressing the coronavirus.

Exercise Overview

COVID-19 was one of the largest issues in 2020 from a medical, technological, political, and social point of view.

By keeping an open mind and exploring potential solutions, we can contribute to global efforts in addressing this international challenge.

Project Duration

1 week
(April 2020)

Team Size

Design team of 1
(sole designer)

My Responsibilities

Secondary Research, Survey, Storyboarding, User Flow, Prototyping

Tools Used

Pencil & Paper,
Google Forms, Storyboarder, Figma

Design Exploration

During the height of the 2020 pandemic, I was intrigued by the effects of the coronavirus and people's perceptions of it. Being that this was a multi-faceted problem that could be addressed by many potential solutions (through policy, medicine, technologies, etc) I was interested in applying my understanding of user experience to help contribute to easing the effects of this problem.

Initial Research

I conducted secondary research to learn more about COVID as well as preventative recommendations. I followed this research with a survey sent to 29 participants in order to identify their qualitative perceptions in regards to the virus.

Read the research summary here.

Storyboarding

Using the data I collected, I began storyboarding. I followed the jobs-to-be-done framework to identify key areas in which users have unique motivations and expected outcomes given particular situations.

User Flow

After applying the JTBD framework, I ideated on potential features that could help address each job. Given the pervasive nature of a watch interface, I decided to map the user flow within a watch-based application.

Onboarding and Assessment

As part of the onboarding process, users are given a self-assessment outlined by the government of Ontario. This assessment consists of 11 yes/no questions with 4 potential outcomes, and can be accessed at any time within the app.

GPS Risk Indicator

Using contact tracing and GPS, the application will also indicate when the user is near an area of high risk. The user can then click on the risky area to learn more about why the area is risky.

Hand-to-face Counter

Unfortunately, people touch their faces on average 20 times per day without knowing and this could introduce the virus to primary points of entry. Using the built-in gyroscope, the solution can help users be more aware and break this habit.

Proper Handwashing

The survey suggested that users weren't confident in their ability to thoroughly wash their hands. This application can guide users through proper handwashing techniques and promote regular handwashing.

Reflection

By taking on this design challenge, I hoped to design a solution to help address the rising problem of the novel coronavirus. Additionally, I tried to design a solution that explores an interface outside of traditional mobile or desktop interfaces. By exploring a watch-based interface, I could ideate potential features in which the user could regularly track their daily habits effortlessly. In terms of next steps, I hope to test this solution with users in a semi-longitudinal study to gauge its daily use and its effectiveness in tracking habits such as handwashing and face touching.

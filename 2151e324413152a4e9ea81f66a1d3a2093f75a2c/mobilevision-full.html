Mobile Vision

Exploring what our imperative to be mobile-first company means for users and informing digital engagement strategies.

Case Overview

Given the continued growth of mobile as Canadians' primary device, one of Capital One's key imperative was to become mobile-first.

By establishing a customer-centric mobile-first strategy, we can encourage digital engagement and empower users to succeed with credit.

Understanding the Intent

For research throughout the project, we collaborated with an industry partner who gave us access to information regarding their practice. Since we focused on dispute resolution, we looked into the legal separation process in Ontario. These were the key insights in our user discovery process.

Empathy Interviews

We gathered feedback regarding the wireframes through client-walkthroughs and interviews with professionals from our industry partner. With their experience conducting financial disclosures, they had excellent feedback regarding our innovation and insight into how a client might feel.

What Went Well
Our participants liked the idea of simplifying the process to the client in steps and the way progress was always visualized in the interface. They especially liked that users had the option to do the screening interview themselves or with a professional leading them. Finally, they found that autofilling information from the disclosure into legal documents was extremely valuable.

What Could be Improved
Moving forward, we needed to address user's concerns about integrating a client management system into the platform for multiple disclosures, the client's ability to edit and see submitted documents, and the professional's ability to keep track of related activities by collaborators.

After lean evaluation, we jumped right into high-fidelity prototyping to support the team's agile development process. We specifically focused on particular user scenarios.

Reflection

According to a cost evaluation based on competitive analyses, this platform can help the user save 86% of monetary expenses compared to the traditional process. By the end of this project, this platform also secured an official partnership with the Ontario Association for Family Mediation. Along the way, I learned three key things that will extend to my future work:

Keep it Quick, Keep it Agile

Many aspects of user-centered design seem linear; just because we learn about different tools in school doesn't mean we have to use them for every project! Weigh the pros and cons and ship a MVP for quick, iterative prototyping.

Cross-Collaboration is Key

Working in a small team allowed me to communicate with members beyond my scope of expertise. By learning more and more about their work, I was to understand their constraints and requirements to balance user needs with business needs.

Missed the Mark? Do it Again

We felt distraught when we learned that weeks of research and hard work led to a subpar usability score. With a month left to redesign the platform, we applied lean UX principles with quick iterations to find success.

Moving forward with this solution, we would like to analyse the platform longitudinally to verify that it saves time for our users compared to the traditional process. Additionally, we want to continue exploring deeper areas of brand customization to support buy-in from enterprise-level users and professionals who manage small teams on the platform.

Capital One

Helping customers succeed by bringing ingenuity, simplicity, and humanity to banking.

Overview

From 2020 to 2021, I had the opportunity to work as a design strategist and design researcher at Capital One.

I was a contributing member for several large-scale projects (>1M users) and helped establish new remote research methods.

Project Duration

1 year, 4 months
(May 2020 - Dec 2021)

Team Size

Full team of 11
(including me)

My Responsibilities

Primary/Secondary Research, Facilitation, Synthesis, Prototyping

Tools Used

Google Suite, Jamboard, Zoom, UserZoom, Figma

Challenge and Growth

Due to the pandemic, this was the first entirely remote position I held. Especially since so much of my work was based on developing empathy, many activities such as interviews, discussions, meetings, and workshops proved more challenging than usual. Imposter syndrome and introversion were new challenges that I quickly overcame through regular 1-on-1s and collaborating closely with key stakeholders. Over time, I grew confident in my work and proactively sought more opportunities to learn and grow, such as facilitating design sprints and workshops of my own, interviewing customers for high-impact projects, and presenting research findings and strategies to senior leadership.

By the end of my contract, I received a 2020 spot award and the 2021 Beacon Award, the most prestigious award given in Canada! I was recognized after volunteering to help facilitate a design thinking workshop with organizations who partnered with Capital One during the 2020 Digital For Good Summit as well as my advocacy for senior accessibility and digital self-serviceability.

Project Briefs

As a design strategist and researcher, I had the pleasure of working on several projects within multiple teams.
Some of these projects include:

  • Identifying opportunities to promote digital engagement and reducing call-related expenses by ~$100k annually
  • Ideated a strategy to motivate customers to pay their minimum balances at a time of financial hardship due to COVID
  • Conducting research and advocating for inclusive design with regards to seniors' digital banking experience
  • Completing historical research audits and competitive analyses to evaluate the company's mobile banking strategy
  • Planning discussion guides and moderating remote research for various usability and empathy interviews
  • Facilitating design sprints to collaboratively ideate a card locking functionality within the mobile app

Due to the nature of NDAs, I cannot disclose specifics about these projects within my portfolio. If you'd like to learn more about the work I did at Capital One, feel free to reach out to me at my LinkedIn or my email!

Reflection

Reflecting on my time at Capital One, I realize that this experience had a profound effect on my personal and professional growth. I had the opportunity to try new things, tackle complex problems, and learn from a team of talented professionals. I was constantly challenged with new and exciting tasks that solidified my passion for user advocacy and design. With the help of my mentors, I became much more confident in my work and learned to always question not only if we were designing the right solutions, but rather if we were focusing on the right problems. Despite the challenges of a work-from-home environment, the connections I made and the lessons I've learned made this experience unforgettable.
